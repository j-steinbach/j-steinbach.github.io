---
title: Thomas and the fire
---

Crackle, crackle. I watch the fire spread in the garden. From the bushes to the roses, from the hedges to the trees. Crackle crackle, sparkle sparkle. From the gras to the curtains, from the curtains to the wooden beams. The whole house burns. Bursts in flames, the roof aflame. From the roof to the clouds, from the clouds to the world. The whole world is aflame. I go to sleep.

I pull down my curtains, lay in bed and watch the lights dance on my wall. Everything is good.

---

'Tell me about your dreams.', she asks. Like every time. I stare at my finger. It blocks her out of my view, I only see her silouhette in the armchair. With her brown hair sticking out, where my nail is.

'Tell me about your dream yesterday. You mentioned a fire?'

I move my finger across the room. Everywhere it touches burns. The soothing paintings on the wall, the medical diplomas, the wooden cabinets, the carpet. The world is on fire again.

'Thomas? Are you there? Thomas?'

The world is no longer aflame. I stop looking at my finger, start looking at her. Cream colored skirt, white top, some stockings maybe. I slowly move my finger again. Her top starts to burn, then her skirt. She doesn't notice. I can see her bra, her panties. She doesn't care, continues talking. Now the fire spreads to her legs and she is naked. Beautiful. Both her body and the flames. Her hair burns, her breasts burn, her skin burns. Flesh melts, tissue melts, muscles melt. A skeleton sits there and grins at me. It talks.

'I am going to stop here today. You are not present, again. I know that it is difficult right now, with Claire gone, but that is why you need to keep trying, you need to be present.'

Claire.

My cat.

My love.

My everything.

How could I forget?

How could I sleep? Breathe? Be alive?

I start to cry.

Claire.

How could you leave?

'Do you need a tissue?', Claire asks.

I nod. Start smiling. She is still here.
